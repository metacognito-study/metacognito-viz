Real vs Ideal Gas - the compressibility factor Z = PV / nRT

For an ideal gas Z = 1 at every pressure (flat line). Real gases deviate: at low P, attractions pull molecules together → Z dips below 1; at high P, the finite size of the molecules dominates → Z rises above 1. Curves come from the van der Waals equation. R = 0.08206 L·atm·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹
